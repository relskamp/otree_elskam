This page illustrates how the auction works and how profits are calculated. In this example, there are two bidders (Bidder A and Bidder B) each with a demand for two units who are competing in market in which there are three units of the same good up for sale.

Suppose Bidder A recieves randomly assigned values of $63 and $44 and Bidder B recieves randomly assigned values of $56 and $48.

Assigning Values

# Value Bidder
1{{63|c}}A
2{{44|c}}A
3{{56|c}}B
4{{48|c}}B

Now suppose Bidder A bids $58 for the first unit and $20 for the second unit she demands. Similarily, Bidder B bids $50 for the first unit and $28 for the second unit she demands.

Placing Bids

# Bid Value Bidder
1{{58|c}}{{63|c}}A
2{{20|c}}{{44|c}}A
3{{50|c}}{{56|c}}B
4{{28|c}}{{48|c}}B

Once all the bids are submitted, the computer will sort the bids from highest to lowest. As you see in the table below, the first three highest bids have won the units. The fourth highest bid lost the item and is highlighted in pink. All bidders that won an item will have to pay their bid for that unit won.

Auction Outcomes

# Status Bid Value Bidder
1won{{58|c}}{{63|c}}A
2won{{50|c}}{{56|c}}B
3won{{28|c}}{{48|c}}B
4lost{{20|c}}{{44|c}}A

In the example depicted in the above Table, Bidder A earned 1 unit while Bidder B earned 2 units. Bidder A’s profits are calculated as

  • Unit 1: $63 - $58 = $5
  • and nothing on unit 2
  • Total profits = $5

Bidder B’s profits are calculated as

  • Unit 1: $56 - $50 = $6
  • Unit 2: $48 - $28 = $20
  • Total profits = $26
