This page illustrates how the auction works and how profits are calculated. In this example, there are two bidders (Bidder A and Bidder B) each with a demand for two units who are competing in market in which there are three units of the same good up for sale.

Suppose Bidder A recieves randomly assigned values of $63 and $44 and Bidder B recieves randomly assigned values of $56 and $48.

Once all the bids are submitted, the computer will sort the bids from highest to lowest. As you see in the table below, the first three highest bids have won the units. The fourth highest bid lost the item. The fourth highest bid is highlighted in pink and sets the market price that the winning bidders will have to pay for the units won.

In the example depicted in Table 1, Bidder A earned 1 unit while Bidder B earned 2 units. Bidder A’s profits are calculated as

  • Unit 1: $63 - $20 = $43
  • and nothing on unit 2
  • Total profits = $43

Bidder B’s profits are calculated as

  • Unit 1: $56 - $20 = $36
  • Unit 2: $48 - $20 = $28
  • Total profits = $64
